For Caden

Packing Checklist

Mom will figure herself out — this one's for Caden. Make sure he has everything before they leave Pittsburgh.

🥾

Hiking Clothes

Comfortable athletic shorts or pants, moisture-wicking t-shirts, a long sleeve layer. Regular running shoes are totally fine for all the hikes on this trip — no need for hiking boots.

🧥

Puffy Jacket

Non-negotiable for Timberline Lodge. It drops to 50–55°F at 6,000ft in the evenings even in July. A packable down or synthetic puffy is perfect — takes up no space in the bag.

🩱

Swimsuit

For paddleboarding on Friday and possible swimming at Skamania Lodge. Bring one — it'll get used.

🕶️

Sunglasses & Hat

UV is intense at altitude on Mt. Hood and the Gorge gets full sun. A brimmed hat and a good pair of sunglasses are must-haves for both the hike and the zip line.

💊

Medications & Sleep Aids

Whatever Caden takes regularly — make sure it's packed in his carry-on, not checked luggage. If he uses anything to sleep on planes or in new places, bring it.

🎧

Devices & Chargers

Apple Watch + charger, headphones for the flights (it's a 5+ hour travel day through Chicago). Phone charger. A portable battery pack is a good call for long hiking days.

For peace of mind

Safety & Emergency Info

Ground rules for the trip and the nearest top-rated medical facilities at every stop — so everyone back home knows the plan.

Ground Rules
🥾
All hikes are a group of three — Ashley, Mom, and Caden together on trail at all times.
No two-person hiking so there's always a capable adult present in case of emergency.
🪂
Zip line and aerial park at Skamania use professional harness systems with trained guides.
All 3 can participate together. Full safety briefing provided at the activity. Guides are first-aid trained.
📱
Cell coverage is limited above Timberline Lodge. Download offline maps before leaving Portland.
Timberline Lodge has WiFi and a landline. Lodge staff are trained in emergency response at altitude.
🌡️
Timberline sits at 6,000ft — temperatures drop fast after sunset and afternoon weather can change quickly.
Pack layers for everyone even if it's warm in Portland. Sun protection at altitude is important.

Planning notes

Tips & Reminders

🏔

July at Mt. Hood

High 70s–low 80s in the valley, 50–65°F at Timberline (6,000ft). Pack layers for mountain evenings — it gets cold fast after sunset. Bring sunscreen; UV is intense at altitude even on mild days.

⛺

Glamping at Skamania

Platform tents with real beds and linens — glamping, not roughing it. Still bring a light layer for evenings since the Gorge gets a canyon breeze after dark. The views from the tents are something else.

🌊

The Columbia River Gorge

Strong afternoon winds are the norm in the Gorge — that's what makes it a windsurfing destination. Comfortable temperatures, dramatic walls, and waterfalls accessible right off I-84. Multnomah Falls is worth the 10-minute stop on the way home Sunday.

🐾

Sam on the Road

At 14 weeks her awake windows are longer but she still needs out every 1.5–2 hours when awake. Plan potty stops at trailheads and rest areas. Car rides are reliable nap time — the drives are well-timed to her schedule.

🚗

The Hwy 35 Drive

Timberline → Hood River via Hwy 35 is one of the most beautiful drives in Oregon — the eastern face of Mt. Hood, orchard valleys, and wide open sky. Don't rush it Saturday morning. Hood River to Skamania is then just 20 minutes across the bridge.

✈️

Tuesday Departure

Flight departs PDX at 1:45pm. Leave home by noon — PDX is 25–30 minutes away. The easy Monday pace means Tuesday morning is relaxed. A final breakfast at home before hitting the road is the right send-off.
