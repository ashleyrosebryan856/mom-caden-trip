Planning notes

Tips & Reminders

🏔

July at Mt. Hood

High 70s–low 80s in the valley, 50–65°F at Timberline (6,000ft). Pack layers for mountain evenings — it gets cold fast after sunset. Sun protection and plenty of water for everyone on trails.

🌊

The Gorge in Summer

The Columbia River Gorge gets strong afternoon winds — that's what makes it the windsurfing capital of the Pacific Northwest. Comfortable temperatures, dramatic scenery. Multnomah Falls is best visited early to beat crowds.

🐾

Sam on the Road

At 14 weeks she can handle longer stretches than before, but still needs out every 1.5–2 hours when awake. Plan potty stops at trailheads and rest stops. Car rides count as nap time — use them strategically.

🚗

The Hood River Loop

Timberline → Hood River via Hwy 35 is one of the most scenic drives in Oregon — the eastern flank of Mt. Hood with orchard valley views. Don't rush it. Then Skamania is just across the Columbia Bridge, about 20 minutes from Hood River.

✈️

Tuesday Departure

Flight departs PDX at 1:45pm. Leave home by noon — PDX is a 25–30 minute drive and security is quick. The easy morning pace on Tuesday means no rushing. A final breakfast at home is a good send-off.

🌲

First West Coast Trip

Caden gets Mt. Hood glaciers, the Columbia River Gorge, alpine meadows, a historic WPA lodge, a waterfall-filled gorge, and his first time in Washington state. Not a bad introduction to the Pacific Northwest.
